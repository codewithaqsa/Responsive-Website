our story

Chocolate with the story

Chocolate's rich history begins with the ancient civilizations of Central America. The Maya and Aztec peoples revered cacao beans, using them in ceremonial drinks and even as currency. When Spanish explorers brought cacao back to Europe in the 16th century, it was initially enjoyed as a luxury beverage mixed with sugar and spices. The Industrial Revolution later transformed chocolate into the more accessible and varied forms we enjoy today, including the chocolate bar, thanks to innovations like cocoa powder production and conching. Today, chocolate is a global delicacy, ranging from everyday treats to gourmet confections.

About

Here are the three basic flavors of chocolate: Dark Chocolate: Made with high cocoa content and little to no milk solids, dark chocolate has a rich, bittersweet taste. Milk Chocolate: Contains cocoa solids, sugar, and milk powder, giving it a creamy texture and sweet flavor. White Chocolate: Made from cocoa butter, sugar, and milk solids, it has a sweet, rich flavor but no cocoa solids, which makes it different from other chocolates. Each has its own unique profile and fan base!
